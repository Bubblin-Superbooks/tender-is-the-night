Dick was born several months after the death of two young sisters and his father, guessing what would be the effect on Dick’s mother, had saved him from a spoiling by becoming his moral guide. He was of tired stock yet he raised himself to that effort.

In the summer father and son walked downtown together to have their shoes shined — Dick in his starched duck sailor suit, his father always in beautifully cut clerical clothes — and the father was very proud of his handsome little boy.
